My first assignment was with the 3rd Marine Infantry Battalion at Camp Pendleton, California. One Friday during lunch, a young black Marine knocked at my office door. I could tell from his troubled countenance that he was in trouble.

ABOUT ME

I was born and raised in Ogden, Utah. I graduated from Bonneville High School in June of 1967 and attended Weber State College for a year before serving an LDS mission to Los Angeles, California from June of 1968 to June of 1970. Later, during the Viet Nam War, my draft number (#20) inspired me to enlist in the army as a Vietnamese linguist to avoid being drafted into the infantry.
